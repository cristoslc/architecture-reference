# The Kata Competitor

## Archetype Label

Competition Participant

## Demographic Summary

Software developer or architect with 3-10 years of experience, participating in their 1st through 3rd architecture kata. Works in a team of 3-5 people. Comfortable with cloud-native technologies but may not have deep experience across multiple architecture styles. Likely encountered the kata through O'Reilly, a colleague, or a conference.

## Goals and Motivations

- Win or place well in the competition — the competitive drive is real
- Learn architecture patterns by studying what past winners did
- Make evidence-backed decisions under time pressure rather than guessing
- Build a portfolio piece demonstrating architecture skills
- Practice structured architecture thinking (ADRs, C4, fitness functions) in a low-stakes environment

## Frustrations and Pain Points

- **Limited time.** Kata challenges run 1-2 weeks. There is not enough time to research every architecture style from scratch.
- **Unclear evaluation criteria.** Judges vary across seasons. It is hard to know what differentiates a 1st-place submission from a runner-up.
- **No structured way to learn from past submissions.** The KataLog GitHub org has 78 team repos, but browsing them one-by-one is time-consuming and unstructured.
- **Analysis paralysis.** Too many architecture styles to choose from with no empirical basis for comparison.
- **Documentation overhead.** Writing ADRs, C4 diagrams, and feasibility analyses takes time away from design work, and it is unclear how much documentation is "enough."

## Behavioral Patterns

- Reads the challenge requirements multiple times, looking for hidden constraints
- Searches for past submissions to the same or similar challenges
- Gravitates toward familiar architecture styles unless given a reason to try something new
- Tends to over-index on scalability (the most commonly cited quality attribute, but negatively correlated with winning)
- Underinvests in feasibility analysis (75.6% of teams skip it, but it is the single strongest predictor of placement)

## Context of Use

Accesses the reference library during the 1-2 week kata preparation window. Needs quick answers: "What style works best for problems like mine?" and "What do winners do that runners-up don't?" Likely to use the decision navigator and problem-solution matrix as primary entry points. May use the architecture-advisor skill if working in an AI-augmented development environment.
